Title:"Project Hail Mary" by Andy Weir

Genre:Science Fiction

Rating:★★★★★ (5/5)

Review:"Andy Weir does it again! A space adventure filled with humor, science, and an unlikely friendship that'll make you cheer. Ryland Grace wakes up alone on a spaceship with no memory of how he got there, and what follows is an absolutely thrilling ride. Perfect for fans of The Martian who want another dose of witty, scientifically-grounded space survival."

Title:"Educated" by Tara Westover

Genre:Memoir | Non-Fiction

Rating:★★★★★ (5/5)

Review:"A powerful memoir about breaking free from a survivalist family to pursue education. Westover's journey from isolated mountains to Cambridge is both heartbreaking and inspiring. Her prose is beautiful, her honesty raw. This book stays with you long after the last page."

Title:"The Seven Husbands of Evelyn Hugo" by Taylor Jenkins Reid

Genre:Contemporary Fiction

Rating:★★★★★ (5/5)

Review:"Old Hollywood glamour meets modern storytelling. Evelyn Hugo's life story is captivating, full of love, ambition, and secrets. Reid masterfully weaves together past and present, creating characters you'll think about for days. A stunning exploration of identity, love, and the price of fame."

Title:"Thinking, Fast and Slow" by Daniel Kahneman

Genre:Non-Fiction | Psychology

Rating:★★★★☆ (4/5)

Review:"A deep dive into how we make decisions. Kahneman explains the two systems of thinking in fascinating detail. Dense at times, but invaluable for understanding human behavior and cognitive biases. Essential reading for anyone interested in psychology or economics."

Title:"Mexican Gothic" by Silvia Moreno-Garcia

Genre:Horror | Gothic Fiction

Rating:★★★★½ (4.5/5)

Review:"Atmospheric, creepy, and utterly engrossing. Set in 1950s Mexico, this gothic horror novel builds tension masterfully.
